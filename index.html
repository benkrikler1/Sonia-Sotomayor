Sonia Sotomayor's Bio

Sonia Sotomayor, the first hispanic Supreme Court Justice, has made an impact on our country in her time. It all started in 1979 when she was an assistant district attorney in the New York County District Attorney’s Office. In the District Attorney’s office, she started as a trial lawyer under DA Morgenthau. From there, she moved into the private sector, when she worked to litigate international commercial matters at a law firm.

Federal judge Sonia Sotomayor was born in the South Bronx area of NYC, on June 25, 1954. Her parents, Juan and Celina Baez Sotomayor are of Puerto Rican descent. They moved to NYC to have a better life for their children. Her mother was a nurse at a methadone clinic and her father was a tool-and-die worker. Those occupations provided her family with a stable and modest income.

In 1991, President George H.W. Bush appointed her to the U.S. District Court of New York. Until 1998, she served as the youngest judge of the Southern District.

Additionally to her positions on the bench, Sotomayor also taught undergraduate students at Columbia University of Law and New York University School of Law.
